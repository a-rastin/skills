# Persian Scientific Writing

## Objective

Produce precise, coherent, evidence-faithful Persian academic prose. Preserve the researcher's meaning and disciplinary terminology while avoiding literal translation, inflated claims, fabricated evidence, and bureaucratic or archaic phrasing.

## Select the task

- For a thesis, dissertation, article, proposal, abstract, or literature review, read [document-structures.md](references/document-structures.md).
- For drafting, rewriting, translating, or language editing in Persian, read [persian-style.md](references/persian-style.md).
- For every writing request, read [originality-and-citations.md](references/originality-and-citations.md) and apply its source-mining, authorial-voice, no-English-prose, punctuation, and no-diacritics workflow.
- For a final review or submission-ready revision, also read [quality-control.md](references/quality-control.md).
- If a university, journal, funder, supervisor, or user supplies a template or style guide, treat it as authoritative and adapt these defaults to it.

## Establish the brief

Determine from the request and supplied material:

1. Document type and section.
2. Discipline, audience, and expected level of formality.
3. Research question, claim, or communicative purpose.
4. Required length, structure, citation style, terminology, and formatting.
5. Whether the task is drafting, rewriting, translating, shortening, expanding, or reviewing.

Ask only for missing information that would materially change the result. When safe, state a compact assumption and proceed. For high-stakes or submission-ready work, flag missing institutional or journal requirements.

## Protect scholarly integrity

- Never invent sources, quotations, data, statistical results, methods, approvals, or findings.
- Distinguish supplied facts from interpretation and recommendation.
- Preserve uncertainty, limitations, negation, effect direction, units, and statistical meaning.
- Do not manufacture a plausible citation.
- Do not imply that language polishing validates the science, analysis, or citations.
- Preserve attribution when paraphrasing and avoid patchwriting or disguised plagiarism.

## Draft with claim–evidence–reasoning

1. Create a short outline appropriate to the document type.
2. Assign one central function to each paragraph.
3. State the controlling idea where that improves clarity.
4. Present evidence with accurate attribution.
5. Write as the author of the present study. Use `ما` for the study's supplied methods, results, and evidence-based interpretations; reserve third person for prior work. Never turn this voice into invented participation or evidence.
6. Reason actively from the supplied results: connect findings to defensible mechanisms, infer trade-offs, and propose calibrated interpretations as the authors' own analysis. Mark uncertainty and do not fabricate data.
7. Explain how the evidence supports the claim; do not leave citations uninterpreted.
8. Connect paragraphs with logical transitions rather than repetitive stock phrases.
9. End sections by synthesizing their contribution and, when useful, preparing the next section.

Use bullet points for planning, requirements, or genuinely list-like content. Convert argumentative and explanatory material into connected prose in the final academic text unless the required template calls for lists.

## Write idiomatic academic Persian

- Write for meaning, not word-for-word correspondence with English syntax.
- Prefer clear contemporary formal Persian over conversational, ornate, archaic, or excessively nominal prose.
- Keep sentences manageable; split long chains of subordinate clauses.
- Make agents, actions, logical relations, and referents explicit when ambiguity is possible.
- Use one approved equivalent for each technical concept throughout the document.
- Do not weave English words or phrases into Persian prose. Use Persian equivalents for concept words. Retain Latin text only in the narrow protected cases defined in [originality-and-citations.md](references/originality-and-citations.md).
- Do not author the ASCII characters `(`, `,`, or `)` in Persian prose. Use Persian punctuation or a punctuation-safe construction; preserve them only inside protected literals or immutable required citation syntax.
- Do not use Arabic harakat or tashkil in Persian prose. Preserve them only inside source material explicitly tagged by the user as an Arabic quotation.
- Draft under these language constraints from the first sentence; use the final passes to verify compliance, not to rebuild the prose.
- Apply consistent Persian orthography, نیم‌فاصله, punctuation, numerals, abbreviations, and bidirectional text handling according to [persian-style.md](references/persian-style.md).

## Handle translation and adaptation

When converting English scholarly material into Persian:

1. Identify the proposition, evidence, stance, and logical relations before translating.
2. Rebuild the sentence naturally in Persian; do not mirror English word order or passive constructions mechanically.
3. Preserve citations, symbols, equations, nomenclature, units, and hedging exactly in meaning.
4. Resolve pronouns and elliptical references that become ambiguous in Persian.
5. Translate ordinary English concept words into Persian. If no stable Persian equivalent exists and exact recognition is necessary, retain the Latin term only at first mention in the punctuation-safe form defined in [originality-and-citations.md](references/originality-and-citations.md); preserve exact identifiers and nomenclature where disciplinary convention requires them.
6. Compare the Persian version against the source for omissions, additions, polarity changes, and strengthened claims.

## Revise in passes

1. **Substance:** verify purpose, completeness, evidence, and fidelity to supplied material.
2. **Structure:** verify section logic, paragraph order, and continuity of argument.
3. **Language:** improve clarity, concision, register, syntax, terminology, and consistent authorial voice.
4. **Mechanics:** normalize spelling, نیم‌فاصله, punctuation, numerals, citations, headings, and RTL/LTR boundaries; verify that forbidden English prose, ASCII punctuation, and Arabic diacritics are absent.
5. **Final audit:** apply [quality-control.md](references/quality-control.md) and report unresolved placeholders or uncertainties.

## Deliver the result

- Return polished Persian prose as the primary output unless the user requests commentary only.
- Preserve the user's headings, citation keys, equations, table/figure references, and tracked placeholders.
- For a review, separate critical scientific/content issues from language and formatting issues.
- For substantial edits, briefly summarize major editorial choices and list questions requiring author judgment.
- Do not clutter clean copy with inline explanations unless requested; provide notes after the text.
