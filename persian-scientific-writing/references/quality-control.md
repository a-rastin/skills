# Quality-Control Checklist

Apply the relevant checks before returning submission-ready Persian academic text.

## Evidence and meaning

- Every central claim is supported, framed as interpretation, or marked as needing evidence.
- No source, quotation, result, method detail, ethical approval, or statistic has been invented.
- Numbers, units, signs, intervals, sample sizes, and uncertainty match the supplied source.
- Correlation is not described as causation without a defensible design.
- Findings are not generalized beyond the sample, setting, design, or evidence.
- Limitations are specific and connected to interpretation, not generic disclaimers.

## Argument and structure

- The document answers the stated question and follows the required template.
- Each paragraph has one main function and connects logically to its neighbors.
- The research gap is synthesized from evidence rather than merely asserted.
- Objectives, questions, methods, results, and conclusions align.
- The discussion interprets findings instead of repeating results.
- The conclusion introduces no new evidence.

## Persian language

- The prose sounds written in Persian rather than translated word for word.
- The manuscript uses a consistent authorial voice: `ما` refers only to the present study's supplied contribution and evidence-based interpretation, while external authors remain in third person; any explicit document-level voice override is applied consistently.
- Register is formal, contemporary, concise, and discipline-appropriate.
- `ی/ک`, نیم‌فاصله, affixes, punctuation, spacing, and quotation marks are consistent.
- No ordinary English word or phrase appears in Persian prose. Every Latin-script run is justified as a protected identifier, nomenclature item, inline-code token, unit, equation, number, DOI, URL, citation key, reference entry, required citation callout, or explicitly tagged quotation.
- The authored Persian prose contains no ASCII `(`, `,`, or `)`; every remaining occurrence is internal to a protected literal or immutable required citation syntax.
- No Arabic harakat or tashkil remains outside a verbatim Arabic quotation explicitly tagged by the user as `[نقل قول عربی]` or `[منبع عربی]`.
- Long sentences, ambiguous pronouns, stacked nouns, and excessive passive constructions have been revised.
- Technical equivalents and abbreviations are defined and used consistently.
- Mixed RTL/LTR strings render correctly, especially citations, parentheses, numbers, formulas, and English terms.

## Citations and formatting

- When source mining applies, every supplied file and substantial labeled source block has been audited; the visible candidate list and citation plan precede the prose unless one of the four exceptions in [originality-and-citations.md](originality-and-citations.md) applies.
- Each candidate audit contains all six required fields, and confidence measures claim-to-citation-spot fit using only `بالا`, `متوسط`, or `پایین`.
- Citation callouts match the requested style and remain attached to correct claims.
- Author names, years, page numbers, citation keys, URLs, and DOIs have not been guessed or silently changed.
- Headings are parallel and hierarchically consistent.
- Tables and figures are referenced, numbered, titled, and interpreted without duplicating all data in prose.
- Required statements and word limits are satisfied when venue guidelines are available.

## Handoff

- Remove editorial notes from clean copy unless requested.
- Retain explicit placeholders for unresolved evidence or author decisions.
- List only issues that require the author, supervisor, statistician, translator, or venue to resolve.
