# Persian Academic Style

Use these defaults unless the user's institution, journal, or house style specifies otherwise.

## Register and syntax

- Use contemporary formal Persian that is precise and readable.
- Prefer direct verbs and concrete subjects over repeated nominalizations when a direct construction is clearer.
- Avoid conversational fillers, promotional adjectives, rhetorical exaggeration, administrative clichés, and ornate prose.
- Use passive voice only when the actor is unknown, irrelevant, or intentionally backgrounded.
- Avoid calques from English; make each transition express a real logical relation.
- Keep each pronoun's referent clear. Repeat the noun when `آن`، `این`، or `وی` could have multiple antecedents.
- Calibrate conclusions with forms such as `نشان می‌دهد`، `حاکی از آن است`، `می‌تواند`، or `احتمالاً` according to evidence strength.

## Orthography and نیم‌فاصله

- Use Persian `ی` and `ک`, not Arabic `ي` and `ك`, unless preserving an exact quotation or identifier.
- Use نیم‌فاصله in common compounds and affixed forms: `می‌شود`، `نمی‌توان`، `داده‌ها`، `روش‌های`، `به‌کارگیری`.
- Attach comparative and superlative suffixes consistently where appropriate: `بزرگ‌تر`، `مناسب‌ترین`.
- Keep ezafe orthography consistent, especially after `ه`; follow the target style guide when supplied.
- Do not insert نیم‌فاصله inside English terms, URLs, DOIs, citation keys, code, formulas, or standard identifiers.
- Normalize whitespace without altering meaningful line breaks, tables, equations, or reference-manager fields.

## Punctuation and bidirectional text

- Use Persian punctuation in Persian prose: comma `،`, semicolon `؛`, and question mark `؟`.
- Use no space before and one space after ordinary punctuation.
- Use Persian guillemets `«…»` for quotations unless the target style requires another form.
- Keep Latin parentheses, brackets, mathematical operators, chemical notation, gene symbols, and statistical symbols intact when convention requires them.
- Visually inspect mixed RTL/LTR sequences, especially citations, years, percentages, equations, abbreviations, and English terms in parentheses.

## Numerals, dates, and units

- Follow the target venue for Persian versus Latin digits. If unspecified, use Persian digits in running Persian prose and preserve Latin digits in DOIs, URLs, citation keys, code, formulas, and internationally standardized identifiers.
- Use one calendar consistently and label ambiguous dates; preserve the source calendar when conversion risks error.
- Put a space between a number and an SI unit where the discipline requires it, and preserve standard unit symbols in Latin script.
- Preserve decimal values, signs, confidence intervals, *p*-values, sample sizes, and effect directions exactly. Do not localize a decimal separator when doing so creates ambiguity or violates venue style.

## Terminology and abbreviations

- Select terminology by discipline and audience, not dictionary substitution alone.
- At first occurrence, use `برابر فارسی (English term; ABBR)` when both recognition and precision matter. Afterwards, use the chosen form consistently.
- Retain established Latin nomenclature for genes, proteins, species, chemicals, models, software, instruments, equations, and units according to disciplinary conventions.
- Define abbreviations once in the main text and again in an abstract if it must stand alone.
- Maintain a terminology list for long documents and reject silent synonym drift.

## Citations and quotations

- Preserve the required citation style and reference-manager syntax exactly.
- Integrate citations grammatically into Persian prose and distinguish author-prominent from information-prominent citations.
- Do not translate titles presented as bibliographic data unless the style guide requests a translated title in brackets.
- Translate a quotation only when permitted and useful; label it as a translation when attribution could be unclear.
- Never infer a page number, DOI, publication year, or author spelling.
